Every project needs financing. The ideal solution is to issue tokens and use them to pay developers. But not everyone is ready to accept illiquid tokens as payment.

Investors in a project at the ICO stage often fail to obtain the profit margin, which they expected, due to the actions of the project team. If the product is released late or with inadequate functionality, value of the tokens suffers. Also projects often attract more investments than they actually need. IDO Array solves these problems:

  • Developers receive payment in temporary eRay tokens, which are backed up by Ether at a rate of 1:1

  • Temporary eRay tokens are freely traded on the exchange from the first month after issue

  • Investors invest in stages, exactly as much as is needed for a specific development stage;

  • Anyone can view all project expenditures and the progress being made in real time;

  • The Ray tokens will operate in their own blockchain, which is faster than Ether, but offers the same broad potential for use of smart contracts.

  • After the release of a working version of the product, the eRay tokens are automatically burnt. The Ether that is released is distributed between investors according to their contribution to the project.

Why invest in IDO Array?

  • Receive Ether at a rate of 1:1.

  • Before launch the investor can trade tokens on the exchange or burn his/her tokens and receive Ether at a rate of 1:1.

    After launch a part of the invested Ether can be recovered. When a working version of the product is released, Ray tokens are issued, and the eRay tokens are burnt. The released Ether is returned to investors in proportion to their investments.

The platform idea

Users download the application browser to their computer, which works as a marketplace. After browser installation, the user enters the directory where the distributed application can easily be downloaded and installed. These applications do not require servers, domains, DNS-services and other centralized entities.

For developers, Array makes it easier to develop and run applications. The platform works in popular languages (Solidity and Javascript). Any developer can create and place an application in the Array store of decentralized applications.

The Array platform will be developed and accept investments in stages, and will report expenditures in real- time. Anyone can view information on project progress and expenditures. A working version of the platform will be released no earlier than spring 2019.

1eRay≥1 ETH

At each investment round, eRay tokens are issued in a 1:1 ratio for each invested Ether (ETH). A part of the tokens are taken by investors, and a part provide the budget of the Array project. The project uses tokens to pay its developers, who can exchange them at any time for Ether at a rate of 1:1 (by burning the tokens through a smart contract).

The tokens are freely traded on exchanges from the first month after project launch. If the owner of a token believes that the project is a good investment, he can keep the token or sell it on the exchange.

The distribution of tokens changes gradually during the investment round.

Although, at the time of investment, tokens are issued in an amount equal to the invested Ether, not all of the tokens go to the investor. The ratio varies during the round: at one time most of the tokens may be employed for project needs, and at another time the greater part may be with investors. The ratio changes through a certain number of blocks generated in the Ether blockchain. All investors see the current ratio in real time, the time before the ratio changed, and how much Ether has been collected at the present time.

The offer for investors is more favorable at the end of the round, when the investor receives 90% of the tokens, and the project 10%. The investor choose the block to invest in. However, the financing required to develop the next stage of the project is limited, so the investor risks being too late to take part. For example, if the required amount is collected at the penultimate block (in a ratio of 70% to the investor and 30% to Array), the window for acceptance of investments will close.

Only qualified investors can take part in the project. All investors undergo KYC and AML procedure before they can enter the WhiteList. The current minimum investment is XX ETH.

eRay tokens are burnt as Ray tokens are released.

The residual Ether from eRay tokens is returned to investors. Ray tokens are distributed between eRay holders in proportion to their shares. After the release of a working version of the product, the eRay tokens are automatically burnt. Simultaneously, Ray tokens are issued, which are distributed between the owners of eRay tokens based on their share in the total volume. The Ray tokens are accessed using the same private key as for eRay.

All of the Ether which guaranteed the eRay tokens will be distributed to investors, according to their contribution to the project. By investing now, investors can partially return their investments in Ether, while immediately receiving eRay tokens that are freely traded on exchanges.
